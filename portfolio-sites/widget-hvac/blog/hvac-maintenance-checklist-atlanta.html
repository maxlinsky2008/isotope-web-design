The Atlanta HVAC Maintenance Checklist: Spring and Fall Edition

Atlanta's humidity and pollen load are brutal on HVAC systems. A twice-yearly tune-up isn't optional here — it's the difference between a system that lasts 20 years and one that needs replacing at 12.

Spring Checklist — April, Before Cooling Season

Spring maintenance prepares your system for Atlanta's long, demanding cooling season. Run this checklist in April before temperatures climb above 80°F.

Task Who Notes
Replace air filter DIY Use a MERV 8–11 filter. In Atlanta, replace monthly April–September due to pollen and humidity.
Clear debris from outdoor condenser DIY Remove leaves, pine needles, and mulch from around the unit. Maintain 2ft clearance.
Test thermostat in cooling mode DIY Set to 68°F and confirm cool air comes out within a few minutes.
Check condensate drain line DIY Flush with diluted bleach to prevent algae clogs — a leading cause of summer breakdowns in Atlanta.
Refrigerant charge check Pro Low refrigerant reduces efficiency and can damage the compressor. Requires a licensed technician.
Evaporator and condenser coil cleaning Pro Dirty coils reduce efficiency by up to 30%. Atlanta's pollen makes annual cleaning necessary.
Electrical connections inspection Pro Loose connections are a fire risk and cause erratic system behavior.
Blower motor and belt check Pro A worn belt or unlubricated motor is a common summer failure point.

Fall Checklist — October, Before Heating Season

Fall maintenance prepares your system for heating season and gives you time to address issues before the first cold snap.

Task Who Notes
Replace air filter DIY A fresh filter going into heating season ensures proper airflow and indoor air quality.
Clear leaves from outdoor unit DIY Especially important for heat pumps, which run the outdoor unit year-round.
Test thermostat in heating mode DIY Set to 72°F and confirm warm air within a few minutes. Note any delays or cold spots.
Test carbon monoxide detector DIY Replace batteries. Essential if you have gas heating.
Heat exchanger inspection (gas systems) Pro A cracked heat exchanger leaks carbon monoxide. Non-negotiable annual check for gas furnaces.
Gas valve and ignition check Pro Ensures safe and reliable ignition throughout the heating season.
Heat pump reversing valve test Pro The reversing valve switches the system between heating and cooling. Failure means no heat.

Monthly Tasks Year-Round

These don't require a service call — but skipping them is how small issues become expensive ones.

  • Check and replace filters: Atlanta's pollen count is among the highest in the US. Monthly filter changes April through September are often necessary. A clogged filter forces the blower to work harder and can cause the evaporator coil to freeze.
  • Listen for unusual sounds: Squealing suggests a worn belt or bearing. Rattling suggests a loose component. Clicking that doesn't stop after startup often means a relay failure. None of these fix themselves.
  • Watch your energy bills: A 15–20% unexplained increase in your utility bill often signals a developing HVAC efficiency problem. Compare month-over-month against the same period last year.
  • Keep vents clear: Furniture blocking supply or return vents disrupts airflow and can cause uneven temperatures and system strain.

Signs You Need a Technician Now — Not at Your Next Scheduled Visit

  • Ice forming on the indoor air handler or outdoor unit
  • Water pooling around the indoor unit (condensate drain overflow)
  • Burning smell when the system runs
  • System short-cycling (turning on and off in rapid succession)
  • No cold air despite thermostat set to cooling
  • Unusual sounds: grinding, banging, or persistent clicking
