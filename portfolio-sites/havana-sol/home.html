Nuestra historia

From a Santiago kitchen to your table in Tampa

In 2009, Mireya and Tomás Cordero turned a corner storefront in the Riverside Arts District into a little piece of home. They brought one suitcase of recipes from Santiago de Cuba, a stovetop espresso pot, and a stubborn belief that good food cannot be rushed.

Fifteen years later the plancha hasn’t cooled. Their daughter Lucía runs the kitchen now, the mojo is still squeezed by hand every morning, and on weekends the room fills with the sound of son cubano, the way it does on a porch in Havana.

We’re not a theme. We’re a family, cooking the food we grew up on, for a city we love.
