Most HVAC systems should be serviced twice a year — once in spring before cooling season and once in fall before heating season. In Atlanta's climate, where systems run hard through long, humid summers, annual maintenance at minimum is essential. A tuned system runs more efficiently, lasts longer, and is far less likely to fail on the hottest or coldest day of the year.

A traditional HVAC system uses a separate furnace for heat and an air conditioner for cooling. A heat pump handles both from a single unit by moving heat rather than generating it — in winter it pulls warmth from outside air, in summer it expels heat out. In Atlanta's mild climate, heat pumps are highly efficient and cost-effective for most homes, typically with lower operating costs than gas furnace systems.
